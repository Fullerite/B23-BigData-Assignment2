Ondrej Lenárd (9 September 1942, Korompa, Hungary [now Krompacy, Slovakia]) is a Slovak conductor. He was principal conductor of the Czecho-Slovak Radio Symphony Orchestra from 1977 to 1990 and of the Slovak Philharmonic Orchestra from 1991 to 2001, where his concert work included performances of Ján Levoslav Bella's Wieland der Schmied.Anderson, Robert, "Performance Reviews – Bella: Wieland der Schmied" (December 1993). The Musical Times, 134 (1810): pp. 721–723. His recordings include a Marco Polo issue of the Symphony No. 1 of Havergal Brian,Simpson, Robert, "Record Review - Havergal Brian; Symphony No. 1, 'The Gothic'" (September 1990). Tempo (New Ser.), 174: pp. 41–43. and a Naxos recording of the complete Nutcracker. In 2013 he was the conductor of the 70th anniversary concert held at the Hungarian State Opera of the famed Hungarian soprano Éva Marton, which featured Grace Bumbry and Jonas Kaufmann.
