Ong Eng Hong (; 1935 – 2018) was an Australian badminton player. In 1955, he competed in the Australian National Badminton Championships and made his debut as the champion of the men's singles. ==Early life== Ong was born in 1936 in Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia. His ancestral home is in Jiangdou, a small village in Fuqing, Fujian, China. His father Ong Cho Tek () was a businessman dealing in the early car and bicycle spare parts trade in Singapore and Malaysia. He has 18 siblings. ==Personal life== Ong and his wife, Goh Soh Meng, were married on the 22 December 1962 in St Kilda, Victoria.
