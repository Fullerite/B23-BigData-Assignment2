Orthoclydon pseudostinaria is a species of moth in the family Geometridae. It is endemic to New Zealand. It is classified as critically endangered by the Department of Conservation. ==Taxonomy== This species was first described by George Vernon Hudson in 1918 under the name Xanthorhoe pseudostinaria. The moth was subsequently also found to be present at Gouland Downs near Nelson as well as in the Nelson district, Mount Grey and White Rock in Canterbury. ==Plant hosts== The plant host of O. pseudostinaria is unknown. Given the rarity of the moth species it has been hypothesized that the host plant is also uncommon. ==Conservation status== In 1928 Hudson regarded this species as being very rare and this moth is now classified under the New Zealand Threat Classification system as being Nationally Critical. ==References== Category:Moths of New Zealand Category:Moths described in 1918 Category:Cidariini Category:Endemic fauna of New Zealand Category:Taxa named by George Hudson Category:Endangered biota of New Zealand Category:Endemic moths of New Zealand
